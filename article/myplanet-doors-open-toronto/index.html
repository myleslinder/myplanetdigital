Myplanet opens its doors to Torontonians on May 24th - 25th, 2014.

The 15th annual Doors Open Toronto presented by Great Gulf is a city-wide celebration offering free, rare access to more than 155 architecturally, historically, culturally and socially significant buildings across Toronto.

This year's theme is Secrets and Spirits... exploring the Mysteries Behind the Door. Close to 80 locations feature secret spaces, stories of spirit sightings and unexplained mysteries. Look for the mysterious keyhole icon while browsing the list of participating buildings or walking tours.

About the Bank of Upper Canada building

The Bank of Upper Canada was constructed in 1825 and is a designated National Historic Site of Canada. The building was designed in the Palladian architectural style. This two-and-a-half story Neoclassical building received this designation for the role that the bank played in the development of Upper Canada, the contribution it made to Torontoâ€™s rise as an economic centre and the design of the building. Notable heritage features of the building include neoclassical portico (designed by John G. Howard), a stone exterior that features rusticated masonry on the foundation as well as smooth-dressed stonework on the upper storeys.

Inside, visitors will find a mix of preserved heritage features as well as modern interior design elements. Some of the preserved heritage features include original columns, paneling and interior shutters.
